Professional Career

After graduating from Holy Cross in May 2015, Donny signed a contract with the Gateway Grizzlies of the Frontier League. Donny pitched as both a starter and reliever for the Grizzlies.

Collegiate Career

After attending Boston College High School, Donny went on to play at Holy Cross, where he was a 4 year member of the starting pitching rotation. As a freshman, he won the team's Ron Succi Award for Rookie of the Year. As a sophomore, Donny was selected to the Patriot League All-Tournament team. During his junior and senior years he was named to the All-Conference team. Donny concluded his career ranking in Holy Cross' record books as third all-time in innings pitched (241.0), strikeouts (183) and games started (37).

Craig Schlitter

Pitcher: Colorodo Rockies







Professional Career

Drafted in the 27th round (803rd overall) by the Colorado Rockies. Schlitter spent his rookie campaign in Grand Junction Colorado where he was a reliever posting a 3-1 record with a 2.41 ERA. Schlitter finished his second full season of pro ball in Advanced A (Modesto, California) after a short stint in A ball (Boise, Idaho) where he posted a 2-0 record as a reliever with a 2.25 ERA while striking out 24 batters in 14 innings.

Collegiate Career

A four-year starter at Bryant University, Schlitter finished his career second all-time with a 28-13 record while posting a career 2.67 ERA over 300 innings of work while leading the Bulldogs to back-to-back regional appearances in 2013 and 2014.Schlitter racked up numerous accolades including: First Team ABCA All-Northeast, NEC Pitcher of the Year, First Team All-New England, First Team All-NEC, Baton Rouge Regional All-Tournament Team, NEIBA All-Star, NEC All-Tournament Team, NEC Tournament Most Outstanding Player, Four-time NEC Pitcher of the Week.Schlitter played his collegiate summer ball in the Cape Cod Baseball League where he was a starter for the Falmouth Commodores in 2012 and 2013.

Professional Career

Alex signed with the Toronto Blue Jays as a free agent in June, 2013. He played his first professional season with the Bluefield Blue Jays. He played mostly 2B in Bluefield, where he batted .260 and boasted a .944 fielding percentage.In 2014, Alex continued his rise through the Blue Jays minor league system playing for the Vancouver Canadians and batting an impressive .303. 2015 proved to be another successful season for Alex, after playing just 5 games for the Canadians and batting .333, Alex was promoted to "High A" with the Lansing Lugnuts. Playing both 2B and 3B for the Lugnuts, Alex posted career highs in RBI, BB, and AB.

Collegiate Career

A 4-year starter at shortstop for Holy Cross, Alex set many career and single-season program records. Alex ranks 1st in all-time career records for games started (192), games played (202), runs scored (135), stolen bases (53), and fielding assists (546). He also ranks in the top 3 in 5 other categories. Alex holds 4 single-season records in at-bats, games started, games played, and assists.
